<h3>How I got into video editing</h3>
 Inspired by montages from community members of the game "Toribash", I entered the game's video making community on July 29, 2012 with my first video. It was only 18 seconds long, but a part of a <a href="http://forum.toribash.com/showthread.php?t=365779">weekly</a> series where I would apply what I learned each week about Adobe After Effects. They were crude, but they initiated my thirst for learning about video editing. I loved getting feedback from the community I was a part of since 2008, and growing an active fan base and supportive group of friends. 
 
 Later on, I earned the honor of making the game's <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oXFNqkXn0oQ">trailer</a> for their Steam launch and had my first experience being paid a hefty amount for my work. I used the $1000 I earned from the gig to update my computer setup, and had a geniune belief that I could make a career out of this digital medium.

<h3>The COD Editing Community</h3>
Shortly after getting comfortable making Toribash videos, I started to branch out and make short Call Of Duty edits for my clips. I learned more about visual effects and motion graphics along the way. It took a year before my breakthrough moment, coming in 3rd place with my first <a href="https://youtu.be/RcgupsTvTjw?t=1m34s">OneClipContest entry</a>, for my name to be introduced to a bigger audience of editors. 9 months later I reached <a href="https://youtu.be/Po2R74aa7Mg">900 subscribers</a> and joined my first editing team called Ravaged Filmz. 

It wasn't until July 2014 when I had my second breakthrough in the cod community, joining one of the more renowned editing teams called Verum with my <a href="https://youtu.be/DoeQSBMO-iE">application titled "Inside"</a>. It was the summer and I just graduated highschool, so I was pumping out videos and experimenting with all sorts of ideas, like learning how to code to make my own game modifications to stand out among the competition. 

My third and most proud breakthrough in the community was with my edit titled <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jw0Faxz6v8s">"Acedia"</a>, that gave me full membership status in Verum and made my name popular among editors in the community. At the time I was one of very few making Modern Warfare 2 edits that stood out, I pulled out all of the tricks I learned with game modding to create visuals that were never seen before. At the time I was at 2,466 subscribers.

<h3>Joining Relative Minds</h3>
In the summer of 2015 I joined Relative Minds with my edit <a href="https://youtu.be/8KDzpB6Y1ug">"Desert Drive"</a>. It was surreal for me to be accepted into the most renowned editing team, especially on my first application. It was a controversial acceptance for most in the community, since my scripting knowledge surpassed my editing skills. It was clear to me that the team was interested in me because I could create visuals that nobody else could. It was more about my potential than being the most polished editor in the community, and that meant a lot to me. I went on to make a few more edits over the years, the three I'm most proud of since then are <a href="https://youtu.be/7Asjyqi9mR0">Amen</a>, <a href="https://youtu.be/YJxw-utF8HA">Anagnorisis and Perepetia</a>, and <a href="https://youtu.be/v-mwck2CwMw">Kingdom</a>.

<h3>Shifting Focus</h3>
My focus shifted towards heavily towards motion graphics. I liked making music videos for my friends and intros for the editing community. The skills I picked up from making montages transferred into motion graphics and I started taking it seriously. I didn't pursue college after graduation and went with working part time (eventually full-time) while spending the rest of my time exploring After Effects, working on small video gigs, and learning the business side of motion design. I quit my job a day after my birthday on June 7th, 2017 and spent all of my time working on getting clients and establishing myself in the industry.

<h3>Future Plans</h3>
I was a full time freelance motion designer up until December 2017. By then, I was 4 months into working with <a href="https://streamlabs.com">Streamlabs</a> to design and animate alerts as well as overseeing overlay creation for their library of graphics. At the start of 2018 I was offered a full time position and promotion as Art Director, managing our design team to produce content for the library. 

It's unbelieveable to see the fruits of my labor lead up to where I am now, and where it'll take me in the future. My passion for digital media started with watching montages and turned into a career with the feedback and support from the cod editing community. It was a transformative experience that I'm forever thankful for, and it's a wonderful feeling watching friends in the community apply the skills they've developed to move them forward as well.
