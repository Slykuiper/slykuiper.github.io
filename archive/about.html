About

Art Direction, Motion Design, and Video Editing

How I got into video editing

Inspired by montages from community members of the game "Toribash", I entered the game's video making community on July 29, 2012 with my first video. It was only 18 seconds long, but a part of a weekly series where I would apply what I learned each week about Adobe After Effects. They were crude, but they initiated my thirst for learning about video editing. I loved getting feedback from the community I was a part of since 2008, and growing an active fanbase and supportive group of friends. 2 years later, I had the honor of creating the game's trailer for their Steam launch, which was my first experience being paid a hefty amount for my work. I used the $1000 I earned from the gig to update my computer setup, and had a geniune belief that I could make a career out of this digital medium.

The COD Editing Community

A few months after my start making Toribash videos, I branched out into the Call of Duty editing community. I started to experiment more with visual effects and motion graphics. It took a year before my breakthrough moment, coming in 3rd place with my first OneClipContest entry, for my name to be introduced to a bigger audience of editors. 9 months later I reached 900 subscribers and joined my first editing team called Ravaged Filmz. It wasn't until July 2014 when I had my second big breakthrough in the community, joining one of the more renowned editing teams called Verum with my application titled "Inside". This was in the summer and I just graduated highschool, so I was pumping out videos and experimenting with all sorts of ideas, including learning how to code to make my own game modifications to stand out among the competition. My third and most proud breakthrough in the community was with my edit titled "Acedia", that gave me full membership status in Verum and made my name popular among editors in the community. At the time I was one of very few making Modern Warfare 2 edits that stood out, I pulled out all of the tricks I learned with game modding to create interesting visuals that weren't seen before. At the time I was at 2,466 subscribers.

Joining Relative Minds

In the summer of 2015 I joined Relative Minds with my edit "Desert Drive". It was surreal for me to be accepted into the most renowned editing team, and rare for anyone to be accepted on their first application. It was a controversial acceptance for most in the community, since my editing skills subsided my scripting knowledge which wasn't appreciated at face value. It was clear that the team was interested in me because I could create visuals that nobody else could; it was more about my potential than being the most polished editor in the community, and that meant a lot coming from valued members of the top team. I went on to make a few more edits over the years, the three I'm most proud of since then are Amen, Anagnorisis and Perepetia, and Kingdom.

Shifting Focus

My focus shifted towards heavily towards motion graphics. I liked making music videos for my friends and intros for the editing community. The skills I picked up from making montages transferred into motion graphics and I started taking it seriously. I didn't pursue college after graduation and went with working a part-time job (and later full-time) while spending the rest of my time exploring After Effects, working on small gigs, and learning the business side of motion design. I quit my job after my birthday and spent all of my time working on getting clients and establishing myself in the industry.

Future Plans

You can keep up with my current work on instagram/twitter, or see it published here under the Work section after NDA's clear. My passion for digital media started with watching montages and turned into a career with the feedback and support from the cod editing community. It was a transformative experience that I'm forever thankful for, and it's a wonderful feeling watching friends in the community apply the skills they've developed to move them forward as well.
